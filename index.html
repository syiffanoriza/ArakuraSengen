新倉富士浅間神社 Arakura Sengen Shrine

One of the most popular tourist spots in Tokyo. Located at the foothills of Mount Fuji, in the town of Fujiyoshida in Yamanashi Prefecture. It can be easily accessed by train or bus from Tokyo, and the nearest train station is Shimoyoshida station. A must-visit for anyone traveling to Japan.

Visitors can learn about the history and significance of the shrine while enjoying the unique blend of natural wonder and mystical spirituality that the shrine offers.

Get into The History of the Shrine

Arakura Sengen Shrine traces its origins back to the 8th century when it was established as a place of worship dedicated to the Shinto goddess Konohanasakuya-hime. This deity, often associated with Mount Fuji and the blooming of cherry blossoms, symbolizes renewal, beauty, and the transient nature of life.

Its original location was near the base of Mount Fuji. However, due to volcanic eruptions and subsequent changes in the landscape, the shrine was moved to its current hilltop position in the late 16th century. This relocation not only preserved the spiritual essence of the shrine but also granted visitors stunning vistas of the surrounding area.
